something on top of a proper fork, you're free to publish that fork as your
own release — as long as the fork relationship stays visible and intact, and
this copyright notice is retained.

## Publishing a Release

Without explicit permission from the author, you may **not**:

- Publish or distribute a packaged build (`.ablx`, compiled output, or
  similar) of this project, or a derivative of it, outside of an actual
  linked GitHub fork
- Present a build of this project, or a derivative with only superficial
  changes, as your own original release
- Strip or obscure this copyright notice or the connection to this
  repository from any distributed build

If you've built something on top of this and want to share it: submit it as
a pull request to this repo, or fork it properly and keep the fork visibly
linked. If you want to release it independently outside of those two paths,
ask first — open an issue or reach out. Generally open to it; just want to
be part of that conversation rather than finding out after the fact.
